About the Examination of the application

The Examination stage is where the Examining Authority, the developer and anyone who has registered to have their say will get involved and comment on the proposed development at a number of set deadlines.

This is done in writing using the online form unless there is a need for hearings. If hearings need to take place, they are usually about specific issues that need to be discussed in more detail. Anyone involved in the Examination can attend these hearings.

This stage takes 6 months.

What happens at the Examination stage

The Examination stage is where the Examining Authority will look at the proposed project and ask questions.

Developers, anyone who is registered to have their say, and anyone else involved in the project can all comment on the documents or answer any of the questions at each deadline.

You can send your comments by filling in the online form in the project section of this website.

If you have difficulty using online services, you can also send us information by email or post. You can check the project information in the project section of this website to find contact details for the case team.

What you can do if you have registered to have your say

If you have registered to have your say, you can:

  • check what all other parties have submitted
  • comment on that information before each deadline
  • attend any hearings or meetings

The project documents will be published in the specific project section of this website.

To find out more about what you can do if you are registered to have your say, check our guide for having your say about a national infrastructure project.

If you have missed the deadline to register

You can still look at all of the project information and comment using the form, however there is no guarantee that your comments will be included in the Examination of the application.

There is a process you must follow if you submit comments in this way.
