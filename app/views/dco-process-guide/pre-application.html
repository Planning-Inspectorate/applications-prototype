The Pre-application stage

Pre-application is the first stage of the process. This is where the developer must run a consultation and engage with people and organisations in the area. They must also create detailed documents about the impact the project will have on the environment.

During this stage, people or organisations who want to have their say can get involved by contacting the developer.

About the Pre-application service for developers

The Planning Inspectorate offers a Pre-application service for developers who are getting ready to submit their application to us. This is a free service.

This includes:

  • providing advice about how to prepare an application, helping developers avoid extra cost
  • pre-submission checks and reviews of draft documents
  • checking developers have followed all the required steps

You can learn more about the Pre-application service in the Pre-application prospectus

People or organisations who want to have their say

If you want to have your say about a project at the Pre-examination stage, you need to contact the developer. The developer must run a consultation and engage with the community.

There is a guide with more information about what people or organisations who want to have their say can do at each stage.
