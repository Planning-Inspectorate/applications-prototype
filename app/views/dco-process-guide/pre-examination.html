Preparing for the Examination stage

The Pre-examination stage is where we prepare for an Examination. We will identify an inspector or a panel of inspectors called the Examining Authority and make a plan for the Examination stage.

This involves deciding what the Examining Authority will discuss and what questions it might need to ask. We will also plan out what happens during the Examination, if we need to arrange any meetings and each of the deadlines for comments.

Registering to have your say

You must register during the Pre-examination stage to make sure your views are included in the Examination of the application.

The registration for each project will be open for at least 30 days to give you time to register. The deadline for registering will be in the developer's advert, or you can check the project page

There is a separate guide with more information about the steps in the process for people or organisations who want to have their say about a national infrastructure project.

The Preliminary Meeting

In the months after the registration for having your say closes, the Examining Authority will hold a Preliminary Meeting.

Several weeks before this meeting, a letter called the Rule 6 letter is sent to everyone who registered to have their say. This is to let everyone know when and where the meeting will be held. It will also include a draft timetable for the Examination of the application.

You cannot give your views about the proposed development at this meeting.

You can talk about

  • anything that may affect your ability to attend hearings or meet deadlines, for example other local events happening at the same time or issues with travel or working patterns
  • if there are any suitable local places to hold the hearings
  • if there are any groups of people who need a different approach to be able to take part in the process
  • any other matters relating to the draft Examination timetable

What happens next

A week after the Preliminary Meeting, the Planning Inspectorate will publish the meeting notes on the project section of the website.

Everyone involved in the Examination will be contacted to let them know when they can submit further comments and what to do next.

A letter called the Rule 8 letter is sent to everyone who registered, with details of the Examination including the timetable and all deadlines for comments.
