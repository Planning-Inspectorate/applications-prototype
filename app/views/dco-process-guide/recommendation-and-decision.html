About the recommedation and decision

In this stage we write our recommendation report. This must be completed and sent to the Secretary of State within three months of the end of Examination.

The decision making department for the Secretary of State will then use our recommendation report to make the final decision.

What the Planning Inspectorate does to make the recommendation

After the Examining Authority has completed all its enquiries and understood everyone's views, it will then write its recommendation report.

The Examining Authority has three months to write its report.

Who makes the final decision

The Secretary of State for the relevant government department will read our recommendation report. They may also carry out their own consultation if they feel it is needed.

They then make the final decision about if they should grant a Development Consent Order (DCO) for the developer to build their national infrastructure project.

They can take up to three months to decide, and the Planning Inspectorate publish the government's final decision on our website.
