After the decision is made

After the Secretary of State has made their decision, there is a 6 week period where people can challenge the decision in the High Court. This is called a Judicial Review.

About the Judicial Review

If you or another organisation decide to appeal against the Secretary of State's decision, you can ask for a Judicial Review. A judge will be appointed to hear your case in the High Court. The judge's decision will be final.
