Advice notes

Advice notes inform industry and members of the public about a range of planning issues throughout the planning application process under the Planning Act 2008 (PA2008).

Advice notes include:

  • guidance on what to include in an application
  • how the process works
  • what you need to do and when

Advice notes

  • One: Local impact reports

    Advice on the creation of Local Impact Reports (LIR) and includes how it works, the timetable to operate by and what to include in the LIR.

    Version: 2
    Last updated: April 2012
  • Two: The role of local authorities in the development consent process

    Advice on the role of local authorities at different stages of the Planning Act 2008 process. This includes an overview of the process, the local authority’s role and how to get in touch at various stages.

    Version: 1
    Last updated: February 2015
  • Three: Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) consultation and notification

    This advice explains how the planning inspectorate identifies consultation bodies to be notified under Regulation 11 of The Infrastructure Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) Regulations 2017 (the ‘EIA Regulations’).

    Version: 7
    Last updated: August 2017
  • Four: Section 52

    Find out how to apply to the planning inspectorate to authorise an applicant to serve a written notice.

    Version: 6
    Last updated: March 2017
  • Five: Section 53 Rights of entry

    Read advice on applying for a right of entry to gain access onto land in order to carry out surveys.

    Version: 6
    Last updated: March 2017
  • Six: Preparation and submission of application documents

    Guidance for applicants on how to prepare, organise and submit applications to the planning inspectorate.

    Version: 11
    Last updated: May 2018
  • Seven: Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)

    Guidance for applicants on how to prepare the Environmental Statement.

    Version: 7
    Last updated: June 2020
  • Eight: Planning process overview

    Read a step-by-step overview through the planning process for Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects.

    Version: 3
    Last updated: December 2016
  • Nine: Rochdale Envelope

    This advice note addresses the use of the ‘Rochdale Envelope’ approach under the Planning Act 2008 (PA2008).

    Version: 3
    Last updated: July 2018
  • Ten: Habitat Regulations Assessment

    This advice refers to the Habitats Regulations Assessment (HRA) process. Find out what information needs to be provided with a DCO application with respect to HRA at each stage of the Planning Act 2008 process.

    Version: 9
    Last updated: August 2022
  • Eleven: Working with public bodies in the planning process

    This advice explains the framework which governs the involvement of relevant consultees and consenting bodies.

    Version: 4
    Last updated: November 2017
  • Twelve: Transboundary Impacts and Process

    This advice note explains the roles and responsibilities of the Secretary of State, the planning inspectorate, European Economic Area Member States and applicant applicable under Regulation 32 of The Infrastructure Planning (Environmental Impact Assessment) Regulations 2017.

    Version: 6
    Last updated: December 2020
  • Thirteen: Preparation of a draft Development Consent Order and Explanatory Memorandum

    This advice note has been prepared to provide some assistance to applicants when preparing their draft Development Consent Order and Explanatory Memorandum.

    Version: 3
    Last updated: February 2019
  • Fourteen: Compiling the Consultation Report

    This advice note is aimed primarily at applicants and local authorities, however, it is also relevant to any consultee who may be interested to know how an applicant’s Pre-application consultation is reported. It seeks to provide advice about the format and content of the Consultation Report.

    Version: 3
    Last updated: February 2021
  • Fifteen: Drafting Development Consent Orders

    This advice note sets out advice from the planning inspectorate on the preparation of the draft Development Consent Order (DCO). It also reflects the views, on DCO drafting matters, of those government departments that are most involved in the consenting of DCOs.

    Version: 2
    Last updated: July 2018
  • Sixteen: How to request a material change

    The purpose of this advice note is to provide information to applicants about how to request a material change to an application after it has been accepted and before the close of the Examination.

    Version: 2
    Last updated: March 2018
  • Seventeen: Cumulative effects assessment

    This advice note sets out a staged approach to cumulative effects assessment (CEA) for Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects and provides template formats for documenting the CEA within an applicant’s Environmental Statement. Please note, this advice note refers to Appendix 1 and Appendix 2 as separate documents.

    Version: 2
    Last updated: August 2019
  • Eighteen: The Water Framework Directive

    This advice note explains the roles of applicants, the appropriate agencies and the Secretary of State in meeting the requirements of The Water Environment (Water Framework Directive) (England and Wales) Regulations 2017, as applicable to Nationally Significant Infrastructure Projects under the Planning Act 2008. The advice note also introduces and explains the use of Water Framework Directive overview matrices [see Appendix 1] prepared by the planning inspectorate.

    Version: 1
    Last updated: June 2017
