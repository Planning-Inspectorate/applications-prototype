Project timeline

Pre-application Completed

This is where the developer must run a consultation with people and organisations. You can get involved at this stage by finding the developer's website.

Review of application Completed

This is called the Acceptance stage. This is when the developer sends us their application, including the consultation they have carried out. We have 28 days to make this decision.

Pre-examination Completed

We identify an inspector or a panel of inspectors called the Examining Authority and make a plan for the Examination stage. Anyone who wants to have their say will be able to register at this stage. Registration is open for 30 days.

Examination of the application In progress

The examination stage is where the Examining Authority, the developer and anyone who has registered to have their say will get involved and comment on the proposed development at a number of set deadlines. This stage takes six months.

Recommendation and decision Not started

Recommendation and decision is where the Examining Authority writes its recommendation report. This must be completed and sent to the Secretary of State within three months of the end of Examination. The relevant Secretary of State then makes the final decision.
