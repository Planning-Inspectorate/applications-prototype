Register to have your say about a national infrastructure project

Use this service to have your say about a national infrastructure project. We need your personal details and your comments about the project.

Writing your comments

You must include comments with your registration. These will be used by the inspector or panel of inspectors, known as the Examining Authority, to decide what to focus on during the Examination of the application.

Your registration comments (also known as a relevant representation) should relate to the application. You must include a summary of points which you agree and/or disagree with about the application, highlighting what you consider to be the main issues and impacts.

Information about how you think a project may affect your day-to-day life may also be of interest to the Examining Authority.

We need to know anything you think is important to tell us about the proposed project, in as much detail as possible

This information will be:

  • used by the Examining Authority to decide if they recommend the project goes ahead
  • published on our website

You will be able to submit further comments during the Examination of the application once you have registered.

Before you start

If you are registering yourself as an individual

You will need your:

  • full name
  • address
  • email address and telephone number
  • comments about the project

If you are registering on behalf of an organisation or charity you work or volunteer for

You will need your:

  • full name
  • the name of the organisation or charity you work for
  • your work address
  • your work email address and telephone number
  • comments about the project

If you are registering on behalf of another person or organisation

You will need your:

  • full name
  • address
  • email address and telephone number
  • the details of the person or organisation you are representing
  • their comments about the project
