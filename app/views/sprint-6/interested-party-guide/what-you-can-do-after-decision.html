After the decision is made

After the relevant Secretary of State has made their decision, there is a six-week period where people can challenge the decision in the High Court. This is called a Judicial Review.

About the Judicial Review

If you or another organisation decide to appeal the Secretary of State's decision, you can ask for a Judicial Review. A judge will be appointed to hear your case in the High Court. The judge's decision will be final.

You can find out more about the Judicial Review process and how it works.
